Scaling access to

energy


We are a multi-donor results-based financing (RBF) facility established to accelerate and significantly scale up energy access across Sub-Saharan Africa and beyond. We achieve this by supporting clean energy companies in deploying solutions that foster socio-economic development, aligning with both SDG7 and the Paris Agreement.

A global problem

6 out of 10 people in
sub-Saharan Africa are without electricity

This hinders almost every aspect of life, from running your own business and home to accessing quality healthcare and education.

Watch video

An Ambitious
Solution

1.3m

Clean Energy
Connections

Rural electrification unlocks significant socio-economic benefits. Public institutions like health facilities and schools can extend operational hours, improving access to services and education. This, coupled with the ability for businesses, including those run by women, to operate for longer periods and utilize electrical equipment, creates a ripple effect. The local economy thrives, fostering new opportunities and paving the way for a more prosperous future.

20

Individual
connections

This means 100k people can stay up late talking with their families after a long day of work.

300k

Clean Cooking
Solutions

Delivering 300,000 clean cooking solutions to rural areas is a triple win. It combats climate change by reducing harmful emissions from traditional cooking and protecting forests. It decreases dependence on polluting fossil fuels by utilizing cleaner options like solar power. These solutions significantly improve community health by minimizing indoor air pollution and fire risks, leading to safer and healthier homes.

4.8

MtCO2e of Carbon Emissions
Reduced by 2027

Reducing carbon emissions through clean energy is crucial for humanity's sustainable development (SDG7). Cleaner energy sources power our lives without polluting the planet. This protects our health, ecosystems, and future economic opportunities, aligning with the Paris Agreement's goals to combat climate change.

Mini-Grid

Universal Energy Facility to grant up to USD 10.4 million for renewables in Democratic Republic of the Congo, Madagascar and Sierra Leone

The Universal Energy Facility (UEF) a multi-donor results-based financing facility managed by Sustainable Energy for All (SEforALL) is signing several grant agreements with mini-grid companies in the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC), Madagascar and Sierra Leone in the coming weeks. The grantees will receive financial support to build solar mini-grids in communities without access to clean energy and where other polluting energy sources, such as diesel and petrol generators, charcoal and firewood, are common.
